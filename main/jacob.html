Jacob Cole

Venture Partner

Jacob Cole is on a mission
to realize the Internet’s untapped potential for collective intelligence and global inclusion.

While studying computer science and philosophy at MIT and Oxford, his research at the MIT Decentralized Information Group, exposed him to Tim Berners-Lee’s vision for Web: "There are millions of scientists trying to cure the likes of AIDS and Alzheimer's. Maybe the cure is currently separated in different people's heads. How can we design the web so that these half-formed solutions can come together?”

This led him to found IdeaFlow.io, a company building software that enables him scale his systematically connect people with related ideas relevant resources, teams to integrate their collective knowledge into a structured graph, backed by the former CTO of Reddit and the director of the MIT Media Lab.

He is also a core contributor or founder to dozens of Web tools, communities, and knowledge bases, which collectively have millions of users, including Admitsphere, a free wiki of college application advice used by students in over 100 countries, and the first tele-hackathon between Tanzania and MIT.
